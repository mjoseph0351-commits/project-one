Deaf Ministry

Lets talk about the Deaf Ministry

The Gitimbine United Methodist Church, situated in Meru town- kenya, has always been committed to serving its community inclusively and compassionately. In recent years, there has been a noticeable increase in the number of deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals participating in church activities and within the broader community. Recognizing the importance of accessible spiritual ministry for all members, the church embarked on a journey to establish a dedicated Deaf Ministry‚Äîaimed at fostering inclusivity, spiritual growth, and active participation of deaf and hard-of-hearing persons in church life.

What about its origin

What about our motivation

The idea to start a Deaf Ministry was initiated by Joel, a dedicated member of the congregation, at the beginning of 2025. Joel‚Äôs passion for inclusivity and his awareness of the challenges faced by deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals in accessing spiritual resources motivated him to propose this initiative. His desire was rooted in the belief that everyone deserves an opportunity to experience God‚Äôs love and community regardless of their hearing ability.

Discussion and planning

Following Joel‚Äôs proposal, the idea was brought to the attention of the church leadership and the church council. The council members recognized the significance of the initiative, especially given the increasing population of deaf and hard-of-hearing persons in the church and surrounding community. A series of discussions and planning sessions ensued, involving stakeholders such as church leaders, members of the congregation, and individuals from the deaf community to understand their specific needs and expectations.

These deliberations focused on the development of a comprehensive plan that would include sign language interpretation, deaf-friendly communication strategies, outreach programs, and training for church volunteers and staff. The goal was to ensure that the ministry would be sustainable, impactful, and truly inclusive.

Approval and Formal Establishment

After thorough evaluation and strategic planning, the church council officially approved the establishment of the Deaf Ministry in April 2025. This approval marked a significant milestone, demonstrating the church‚Äôs commitment to inclusivity and social justice. The ministry was tasked with creating programs that would foster spiritual growth, community building, and advocacy for deaf and hard-of-hearing individuals.

Basic Information About YHM

Youth Harvest Ministry is registered under the Government of Nagaland, Gov/Reg.No 6177. This ministry came into existence in the year 2009, according to God's Vision through a humble servant of God Rev. Kilang Jamir.

Under the divine direction Youth Harvest Ministry runs Harvest Leadership & Discipleship Training Institute (HLDTI), which trains and equips believers to walk victoriously and witness Christ effectively.

What is Harvest Leadership & Discipleship Training Institute?

Harvest Leadership & Discipleship Training Institute is an intensive One Year Diploma course. Every subject taught in the institution is thoroughly furnished Biblically. Examination is conducted at the completion of every subject.

Every year training session begins from 8th February and completes the course in the 2nd week of December. At the completion of the course, students are honoured with a certificate and a mark sheet during the graduation programme.

Activities

  • Morning Devotion: Meditating and reading the word of God to bring spiritual growth.
  • Social Work: Light works to teach the importance of work and dignity of labor.
  • Leading Praise and Worship: Students learn songs and lead worship to discover their gifts and talents.
  • Games and Sports: Encouraging teamwork and promoting mental & spiritual well-being.
  • Evening & Sunday Worship: Restoring and feeding spiritual life with the word of God.
  • Children Ministry: Ministering to children from different backgrounds and communities.
  • Practical Outreach Ministry: Church Revival meetings and other practical field ministry.

Purpose

  • To lead young people to Christ for personal Salvation.
  • To Disciple believers, to be victorious overcomers in every aspect of their lives.
  • To equip and assist young people for evangelism.
  • To inculcate in individuals an ever-deepening hunger to know Christ through the study of the word of God and corporate worship.
  • To equip the young people to be an instrument in bringing about a revival in the Church and in society.
